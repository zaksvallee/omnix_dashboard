# ONYX Guard Device & Operational Tiers

## Purpose
Define the ONYX guard-device baseline, operational tiers, mandatory safety monitoring, and rollout path for field deployments.

## Device Platform
Primary baseline device:
- Blackview BV5300 Pro Rugged Phone
- Reference: [Blackview BV5300 Pro Rugged Phone](https://ruggedsa.co.za/product/blackview-bv5300-pro-rugged-phone/)

The rugged Android phone is the primary field terminal for:
- communication
- dispatch interaction
- patrol verification
- incident documentation
- environmental monitoring

Operationally, it replaces multiple tools:
- two-way radios
- standalone patrol scanners
- incident notebooks
- camera devices
- visitor logging books

## Device Upgrade Standards By Tier
All tiers share a baseline rugged-device standard. Higher tiers upgrade where the site requires stronger evidence and intelligence capabilities.

### Tier 1: Baseline Device Standard
Minimum:
- reliable GPS
- stable 4G
- daylight patrol-verification camera
- full-shift battery
- rugged protection
- fast-access carry option (belt/holster)

Recommended accessories:
- belt holster or chest harness
- power bank for long shifts/remote sites
- torch support for low-light patrol areas

### Tier 2: Evidence Upgrade Standard
Recommended upgrades:
- increased storage (internal + SD where possible)
- improved camera quality and stabilization
- improved microphone quality for incident recordings
- stronger mount for evidence capture (chest/shoulder)
- reliable high-bandwidth 4G (or 5G where available)

Optional accessories:
- dedicated body camera (preferred), or secure body-mounted phone harness
- charging docks for handover shifts
- fast chargers for short turnaround rotations

### Tier 3: Intelligence / Live Monitoring Upgrade Standard
Recommended upgrades:
- best available low-light camera
- higher-performance chipset for real-time processing
- 5G preferred where available
- night capability support (lighting/IR accessory path)
- expanded storage for high media volume
- optional rugged supervisor tablet (site dependent)

Note:
Tier 3 is an intelligence capability tier, not just a "better phone" tier.

## Mandatory Wearable Safety Monitoring
All ONYX guard deployments include a paired smartwatch/wearable sensor device.

Minimum capabilities:
- heart-rate monitoring
- activity/movement detection
- paired sync with guard phone

Optional capabilities:
- stress indicators
- motion spike detection
- inactivity detection

Recommended wearable characteristics:
- rugged, water/dust resistant
- 7+ day battery life
- target range: R1,000–R3,000

Wearable signals support AI oversight:
- heart_rate
- movement_level
- activity_state
- timestamp

## Guard Operational Tiers

### Tier 1 — ONYX Guard (Verified Operations Standard)
Baseline professional guard capability:
- PTT/voice communication
- dispatch receive + acknowledgement
- GPS tracking
- status changes (En Route / On Site / Clear)
- panic/emergency alert
- incident photo capture
- control-room push notifications
- NFC checkpoint tagging
- patrol route compliance
- patrol time/location proof
- missed checkpoint alerts
- mandatory patrol verification images

Tier 1 is the baseline ONYX standard, not a premium add-on.

### Tier 2 — ONYX Evidence Guard
Everything in Tier 1, plus:
- body-camera or event video capture
- richer evidence chain
- live or near-live media upload
- enhanced supervisor monitoring
- stronger accountability and incident verification

### Tier 3 — ONYX Intelligence Guard
Everything in Tier 1 + Tier 2, plus:
- advanced environmental monitoring
- multi-signal AI analysis (CCTV, bodycam, patrol images, alarms, drone feeds where permitted, community intel)
- predictive patrol insights
- incident pattern analytics
- threat-awareness intelligence layer

## AI Operational Oversight
AI is a digital supervisory layer across all tiers.

Signals:
- GPS movement
- checkpoint scans
- patrol completion
- patrol verification images
- wearable activity/safety
- incident signals

Alert priorities:
- Low: reminders, low-activity prompts
- Medium: movement anomalies, suspicious-object detections
- High: guard welfare alerts, panic activations, confrontation risk

## NFC Patrol Verification Principle
NFC checkpoint tagging is a core operational control for patrol-based deployments.

Benefits:
- patrol completion proof
- fake check-in prevention
- timestamped evidence
- SLA reporting
- route coverage visibility
- security-gap identification

## Connectivity & Offline Requirements
Connectivity target:
- Tier 1: standard 4G acceptable
- Tier 2: high-bandwidth 4G recommended
- Tier 3: 5G preferred where available

Offline requirements:
- local patrol-verification storage
- incident capture while offline
- queued media uploads
- delayed sync on reconnect

## Access-Control Use Cases
### Stage 1: Phone as Scanning/Intake Device (Immediate)
Supports:
- driver-license scan
- vehicle license-disk scan
- ID document scan
- QR/barcode capture

Operational outcomes:
- visitor check-in
- contractor/delivery logging
- suspicious vehicle capture
- watchlist checks
- repeated-vehicle pattern detection

### Stage 2: Phone in Access-Control Decision Workflow (Later)
Future capabilities:
- approve/deny entry
- gate trigger
- visitor permission validation
- allow-list checks

Requires integration with:
- gate systems
- visitor management
- intercom flows
- resident approval workflows

## Optional Expansion Modules
- drone deployment
- CCTV AI analytics
- perimeter sensors
- LPR
- community-intelligence feeds

## Recommended Rollout
1. Standardize NFC checkpointing + patrol image verification.
2. Add evidence-grade capture as premium tier capability.
3. Build access-control scanning module.
4. Expand into environmental intelligence modules incrementally.

This rollout preserves operational discipline at baseline while scaling ONYX into a full intelligence-led command platform.
